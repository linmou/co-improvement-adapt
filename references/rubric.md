# Process Adherence Rubric (Self-Scoring Guide for Adapted Blueprints)

Score the generated adapted task blueprint on each dimension below.
Use scale: 1 = poor / missing    2–3 = partial / weak    4 = good    5 = excellent / exemplary
Provide one short justification sentence per dimension (evidence from the blueprint text).
Sum scores → total out of 30. Flag for revision if total < 24.

- Task Decomposition
  - Description (framework alignment):
    Clear breakdown into modular subtasks that reveal where human and AI uniqueness can be applied most effectively.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - 3–10 subtasks explicitly listed (not vague or lumped)
    - Each subtask classified as AI-fit, human-fit, or hybrid
    - Evidence of template / AI-drafting principle (e.g., mentions reusable phrasing or auto-generation step)
    - Includes slots for unique human inputs (context, intuition, memory) when appropriate
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]

- Uniqueness Mapping
  - Description (framework alignment):
    Explicit bidirectional leveraging of the listed human and AI uniquenesses, creating tight mutual facilitation.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - At least 3–4 uniquenesses named from each side (drawn from framework list)
    - Clear mapping to specific subtasks or phases
    - Bidirectional facilitation explicitly described:
      - Human → AI: context injection, precise rubrics, constraints, steering rules
      - AI → human: task-focused reflection prompts, controlled sampling handoffs, critique nudges, decision-forcing questions
    - Facilitation patterns feel concrete and copy-paste ready (not just one-liners)
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]

- AI Improvement
  - Description (framework alignment):
    The design enables AI to get better in-use through progressive refinement of context, steering, and rubrics provided by the human.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - Explicit mechanism for mid-loop rubric / context updates
    - Feedback from human clearly loops back to tighten AI behavior (e.g., sampling criteria, weighting, filtering)
    - Evidence of progressive tightening across cycles (fewer clarifications needed, better alignment over rounds)
    - Avoids static / one-shot AI usage
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]

- Human Improvement
  - Description (framework alignment):
    Humans gain in task-specific abilities (domain judgment, pattern recognition, salience detection, decision quality) through repeated solving in complex loops; no direct stretching of rare/high-level abilities.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - Focus stays on task-relevant skills (not creativity, ethical imagination, etc. as primary targets)
    - AI embeds subtle, repeated nudges that exercise task judgment (selection, ranking, assumption challenging, uncertainty handling)
    - Loop complexity is high enough to naturally build transferable task proficiency and complex thinking skills ( like creativity, reflection, decision under ambiguity , metacognition)
    - Includes end-of-loop transfer hooks or reflection on task-ability deltas
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]

- Loop Structure
  - Description (framework alignment):
    Closed-loop design with clear phases: initial alignment → iterative co-improvement → end evaluation & transfer check; repeatable / longitudinal by nature.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - Distinct phases clearly described (initial, core iteration, wrap)
    - Iterative core specifies 2–6 realistic cycles with feedback closure
    - Feedback explicitly refines next round (not just collected)
    - Designed for repeatability across sessions or similar tasks
    - Complexity of task-solving loop supports implicit human skill gains
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]

- Effort Minimization
  - Description (framework alignment):
    Human effort is deliberately minimized while output quality standards and improvement potential are maximized.
  - What to look for (scoring indicators):
    - Human role restricted to high-value steering, context provision, sparse judgment/reflection (< 30% total effort estimate)
    - Bulk execution, sampling, processing, and iteration offloaded to AI
    - Reusable templates, patterns, or agent-friendly hooks included for scalability
    - No unnecessary human micro-management designed in
  - Score: [1–5]
  - Justification: [one sentence]
